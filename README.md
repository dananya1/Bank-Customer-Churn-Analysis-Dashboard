**Overview**

This project focuses on analyzing customer churn patterns in a bank using Power BI. The goal was to study customer demographics, account activity, credit score, geography, and product usage to identify segments with higher churn risk and support retention-focused decision-making. The dashboard was designed to provide an interactive view of customer churn trends and key retention-related KPIs.

**Business Problem**

Customer churn directly impacts a bank’s revenue and long-term customer relationships. The challenge was to identify which customer groups were more likely to leave the bank and understand the patterns behind churn so that retention efforts could be prioritized effectively.

**Objectives**

1.Analyze customer churn patterns across demographics and account-related factors
2.Identify high-risk customer segments based on churn behavior
3.Track key churn KPIs such as total customers and churn rate
4.Build an interactive dashboard for churn monitoring and decision-making

**Tools Used**

Power BI, Power Query, DAX

**Dataset Details**

1.Dataset: Bank Customer Churn dataset
2.Records analyzed: 10,000 customers
3.Key fields used:
Customer ID
Gender
Age
Geography
Credit Score
Balance
Number of Products
Active Status
Credit Card Status
Churn Status

**Steps Performed**

1.Imported and cleaned raw customer data in Power Query
2.Checked and standardized data types for churn-related attributes
3.Created KPI measures in DAX such as:
Total Customers
Churn Rate
4.Built interactive visuals to analyze churn by:
Age groups
Credit score bands
Account balance ranges
Country
Active status
Product holdings

**Key Insights**

1.The dashboard showed an overall churn rate of 20.4%
2.Higher churn was observed among inactive customers
3.Customers in the 51–60 age group showed elevated churn compared to other age bands
4.Geography-based churn analysis helped highlight customer distribution and churn-prone regions
